Kidney Cancer

Kidney cancer (most commonly renal cell carcinoma) is often discovered incidentally on imaging done for another reason — before causing any symptoms. This is actually good news, as it usually means the cancer is caught at an early, localized stage when surgery is highly effective. Treatment for localized kidney cancer typically involves removing all or part of the affected kidney (nephrectomy or partial nephrectomy). For very small tumors, active surveillance or ablation may also be options. We'll tailor the approach to your specific tumor size, location, and your overall health.

Common Medications

  • Most medications for kidney cancer (targeted therapy, immunotherapy) are managed by medical oncology
  • Sunitinib (Sutent), Cabozantinib, Nivolumab — examples used in advanced/metastatic disease
  • For localized disease managed surgically, no ongoing cancer medications are typically needed

Before Your Visit

Preparing these items will help us make the most of your appointment time.

  • Bring any imaging (CT or MRI) from outside facilities — bring a disc or have images sent in advance.
  • Note any symptoms: blood in urine, flank pain, unintentional weight loss.
  • Bring a list of all medical conditions and medications — kidney function and overall health shape treatment options.
  • Think about your priorities: preserving kidney function vs. simplest operation vs. fastest recovery.
  • Ask about genetic testing if you have multiple kidney tumors or a family history of kidney cancer.

After Your Visit

Key next steps and what to focus on.

  • After surgery: follow all activity restrictions; avoid heavy lifting for 4–6 weeks.
  • Monitor the remaining kidney — stay well hydrated and avoid NSAIDs (ibuprofen, naproxen) which can stress kidneys.
  • Follow your surveillance schedule (CT or MRI and lab work) exactly as prescribed — kidney cancer can recur.
  • Eat a kidney-friendly diet: limit salt, stay hydrated, moderate protein intake.
  • Blood pressure control is especially important with one kidney or reduced kidney tissue.

When to Call the Office

Call us at (206) 223-6772 if you experience any of the following:

  • Fever, wound redness, or drainage after surgery.
  • Significant blood in urine at any time.
  • Worsening flank pain.
  • New lumps, unexplained weight loss, or bone pain — signs that warrant urgent evaluation.
